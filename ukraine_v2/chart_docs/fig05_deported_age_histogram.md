# Fig 05 — Deported Group: Age at Death Histogram

**File:** `charts/fig05_deported_age_histogram.png`

## What this chart shows
A histogram zoomed in on just the deported group (V2.3: n=195, was n=75 in V2.1). Shows how many people in this group died at each age band. The mean is marked with a vertical dashed line.

## Key finding
Mean age at death = **48.35 years** for the deported group (median = 45 yrs). The distribution is strongly left-skewed — it peaks in the 40s and falls off sharply. There are very few deported individuals who survived into their 70s-80s.

The group size more than doubled from V2.1 (n=75 → n=195) after V2.2 date recovery + V2.3 corrections. The mean barely moved across versions, confirming the original finding was representative despite the smaller sample.

## What to look for
- The peak of deaths is in the 40s — people cut down in their prime
- The right side (old age) is very thin — few deportees survived long enough to die naturally
- The mean line at ~48 reflects the 1937 Sandarmokh/Terror cluster pulling it down
- 35.5% of the entire deported group died in a single year (1937) — 65 of 183

## Why a separate chart for deported?
The deported group's distribution is so different from the others that it gets lost when shown together. This chart lets readers see the shape clearly without the scale being distorted by the larger groups.
