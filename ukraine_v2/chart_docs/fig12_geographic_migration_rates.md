# Fig 12 — Geographic Migration Rates

**File:** `charts/fig12_geographic_migration_rates.png`

## What this chart shows
Where the migrants went — destination countries or regions for the 927 people in the migrated group. Either a bar chart of destination frequencies or a map-style visualisation.

## Key finding
The diaspora concentrated in Western Europe and North America — primarily Germany, USA, France, UK, and Canada. This matches the historical record of the post-WWII Ukrainian diaspora (DPs — displaced persons — who settled in Western occupation zones and were resettled abroad).

## What to look for
- The top destinations — Germany, USA, France dominate
- Secondary destinations reflect specific historical waves (e.g., Prague had a pre-WWII Ukrainian intellectual community; Vienna was an interwar hub)
- Any destination in another Soviet republic would be an internal_transfer, not migrated — so all entries here should be Western countries

## Historical context
Most Ukrainian creative workers who emigrated did so during two waves:
1. **Interwar (1917-1939):** Left after the Ukrainian People's Republic fell to the Bolsheviks
2. **WWII / DP era (1941-1948):** Fled the Nazi or Soviet occupation, ended up in DP camps in Germany/Austria, then resettled in USA/Canada/UK/Australia
