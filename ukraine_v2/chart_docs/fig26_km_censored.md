## What this figure shows

Kaplan-Meier survival curves for all four migration groups using the full extended dataset (N=15,218), including 6,575 right-censored observations (living individuals censored at their current age in 2026). Tick marks on the curves indicate censoring events.

- Y-axis = proportion surviving (P(survive to age X))
- X-axis = age in years (0–110)
- Shaded bands = 95% confidence intervals
- Tick marks on curves = right-censored observations

---

## Key differences from Figure 2 (complete-case KM)

- Non-migrated group now extends with a flatter tail beyond age 70 due to right-censored living workers
- All other groups (migrated, internal transfer, deported) are identical to Figure 2 — all observations are dead
- The deported curve's sharp early decline is unchanged

---

## Interpretation

The extended KM curves visualise the full observed survival distribution including living individuals. The non-migrated group's survival curve is now informed by 6,575 right-censored individuals who have survived at least to their current age (2026 − birth year). The gap between migrated and non-migrated narrows in this representation, reflecting that many contemporary living non-migrants are observed at ages comparable to or exceeding the typical migrant age at death.
