# fig26 — Kaplan-Meier Survival Curves with Right-Censored Data

## What this figure shows

Kaplan-Meier survival curves for all four migration groups using the full extended dataset (N=15,053), including 6,314 right-censored observations (living individuals censored at their current age in 2026). Tick marks on the curves indicate censoring events.

- Y-axis = proportion surviving (P(survive to age X))
- X-axis = age in years (0–110)
- Shaded bands = 95% confidence intervals
- Tick marks on curves = right-censored observations

---

## Key differences from V2.3 (old fig26)

- V2.3 had all censoring concentrated in non_migrated (52.2% vs 0% in all others)
- V2.4: Censoring distributed across all groups (migrated 19.8%, non_migrated 48.9%, internal_transfer 11.7%, deported 6.1%)
- Total N changed: 15,218 → 15,053 (after filtering implausibly alive and null durations)
- All groups now show tick marks for censoring events

---

## Interpretation

The extended KM curves visualise the full observed survival distribution including living individuals. With properly distributed censoring, the survival gap between migrated and non_migrated is more accurately estimated. The deported group's sharp early decline (concentrated deaths 1937–1945) is unchanged.
