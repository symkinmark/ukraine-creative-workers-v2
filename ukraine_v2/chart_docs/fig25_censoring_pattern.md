## What this figure shows

Stacked bar chart showing, for each migration group, the proportion of individuals with confirmed death dates (event_observed=1) versus those right-censored as alive in 2026 (event_observed=0). Extended dataset: N=15,053 (V2.4).

- Red = Dead (confirmed death date in ESU)
- Blue = Alive (right-censored at current age, 2026)

---

## Key values (V2.4 — correct distribution)

| Group | n | % Dead | % Censored |
|-------|---|--------|-----------|
| Migrated | 1,646 | 80.2% | 19.8% |
| Non-migrated | 11,898 | 51.1% | 48.9% |
| Internal Transfer | 1,313 | 88.3% | 11.7% |
| Deported | 196 | 93.9% | 6.1% |

---

## Key difference from V2.3 (old fig25)

In V2.3, all 6,563 living individuals were incorrectly assigned to non_migrated, producing 0% censoring in migrated/internal_transfer/deported. This made the Cox model structurally invalid. V2.4 properly classifies living individuals using the same AI pipeline as the dead cohort.

---

## Interpretation

Censoring is now distributed across all groups, reflecting the actual migration distribution of the living cohort. Non-migrated remains most censored (48.9%) because it is the largest group. The migrated group has 19.8% censoring — consistent with there being living migrants in the modern Ukrainian diaspora.
