# Fig 22 — Creative Workers vs Estimated Educated Urban Ukrainian Population

**File:** `charts/fig22_educated_urban_comparison.png`

## What this chart shows
Same layout as fig21, but instead of Soviet republic comparisons, this overlays an estimated "educated urban Ukrainian LE" band — based on the Shkolnikov et al. (1998) educational mortality gradient: university-educated people live approximately +3 to +5 years longer than the general population average. Note: "LE" here refers to published demographic life expectancy (e₀), not our metric of mean age at death.

The shaded band represents the range: Ukrainian SSR general LE (e₀) + 3 yrs (lower bound) to + 5 yrs (upper bound).

## Key finding (V2.3 — migrated n=1,280, mean age at death 75.25 yrs; non-migrated n=6,030, mean age at death 71.22 yrs)
- The **migrated group** (mean age at death) approaches the upper edge of the educated urban LE (e₀) band by the 1980s (age 77 vs estimated ceiling ~75-78)
- The **non-migrated group** (mean age at death) sits inside or slightly below the band — meaning they did not fully capture the educated-class survival premium, likely due to Soviet conditions suppressing it
- The **deported group** is far below the educated-urban baseline in every period (mean age at death 48.35 yrs)

## What to look for
- The shaded band is not hard data — it's a constructed estimate based on published e₀. The footnote makes this clear.
- Whether non-migrated (mean age at death) sits inside or below the band: if below, it suggests Soviet conditions reduced the survival premium that educated people normally enjoy
- The migrated group eventually surpassing the band = diaspora in the West achieving higher mean age at death than even educated Ukrainians at home

## Data sources
- Shkolnikov V.M. et al. (1998) Euro J Public Health — educational mortality gradient
- Meslé & Vallin 2003, UN WPP 2022 — Ukrainian SSR baseline
