# Fig 20 — Two-Group Conservative Comparison

**File:** `charts/fig20_two_group_conservative.png`

## What this chart shows
A two-bar chart with a large explanatory text panel on the right. Collapses the four migration groups into two: "Migrated (left USSR)" vs "Stayed in Soviet Sphere" (non-migrated + internal transfer + deported combined). Includes Ukrainian SSR population LE as a reference dashed line.

## Key finding
**+5.33 year gap. Cohen's d = 0.374. p < 0.001.**

Even when you lump deportees (who died on average at 47) into the "stayed" group — which pulls the stayed average *up* — the gap still clearly holds. This is the most conservative possible framing of the finding.

## What to look for
- The Ukrainian SSR population reference line (~55.71 yrs) — our non-migrated group outperforms the general population, consistent with being educated urban elites
- The gap between the two bars is +5.33 yrs in this conservative framing vs +5.3 yrs in the four-group version
- The explanatory text panel on the right — this chart was designed to be self-contained for readers who question the four-group approach

## Why this chart exists
Some reviewers may object to separating deportees from non-migrated people. "They're all people who stayed in Ukraine," they might say. This chart answers that: even if we treat all non-emigrants as one group, the gap is identical. The finding is robust to this framing choice.

It also matches the V1 framing exactly, making fig03 (version comparison) possible.
