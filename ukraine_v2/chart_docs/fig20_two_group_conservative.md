# Fig 20 — Two-Group Conservative Comparison

**File:** `charts/fig20_two_group_conservative.png`

## What this chart shows
A two-bar chart with a large explanatory text panel on the right. Collapses the four migration groups into two: "Migrated (left USSR)" vs "Stayed in Soviet Sphere" (non-migrated + internal transfer + deported combined). Includes Ukrainian SSR population LE as a reference dashed line.

## Key finding (V2.2)
**+4.66 year gap. Cohen's d = 0.332. p < 0.001.**

- Migrated: n=1,272, mean LE = 75.26 yrs
- Stayed (all others): n=7,317, mean LE = 70.61 yrs

Even when you lump deportees (mean LE 47.85) into the "stayed" group — which *raises* the stayed average — the gap still clearly holds. This is the most conservative possible framing of the finding.

## What to look for
- The Ukrainian SSR population reference line (~55.71 yrs) — our non-migrated group outperforms the general population, consistent with being educated urban elites
- The gap between bars: +4.66 yrs in this conservative framing vs +4.03 yrs in the four-group version
- Counterintuitive: lumping in deportees (who died at 47-48) pulls the stayed average *down*, yet the conservative framing still shows only the already-visible gap. This is because deportees are a small fraction (178/8,606 = 2%) of the combined stayed group.
- The explanatory text panel on the right — designed for self-contained reading

## Why this chart exists
Some reviewers may object to separating deportees from non-migrated. "They're all people who stayed in Ukraine," they might say. This chart answers that: even treating all non-emigrants as one group, a +4.66 year gap holds. The finding is robust to this framing choice.

It also matches the V1 framing exactly, making fig03 (version comparison) possible.

## Known issues / improvements
- Chart generated from V2.2 data — numbers in the chart image (+4.66, d=0.332) supersede the old V2.1 values (+5.33, d=0.374).
- The explanatory text box on the right is a good design choice — rare to see this in papers, but very useful for self-contained reading.
