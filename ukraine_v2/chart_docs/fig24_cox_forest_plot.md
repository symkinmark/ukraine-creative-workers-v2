## What this figure shows

A forest plot of hazard ratios (HR) for all three non-reference migration groups (Migrated, Internal Transfer, Deported) relative to the non-migrated reference group, for both Cox Model 1 (unadjusted) and Model 2 (adjusted for birth decade, profession, and region).

- **HR < 1** = lower hazard of death = longer survival than non-migrated baseline
- **HR > 1** = higher hazard of death = shorter survival
- **HR = 1** (dashed vertical line) = null (no difference from non-migrated)
- Error bars = 95% confidence intervals

---

## Interpretation

- Migrated workers have ~24% lower mortality hazard than non-migrated after adjustment — consistent with the OLS +3.31 year coefficient.
- Deported workers have ~5.4× higher mortality hazard — consistent with the OLS −23.44 year coefficient under a non-parametric hazard structure.
- Internal transfer shows a small but statistically significant elevated hazard in Model 2 (HR=1.08, p=0.021), consistent with the OLS Model 2 finding of −1.43 years.
- The fact that all three directions replicate across OLS and Cox strengthens confidence in both models.

---

## Technical note

The Cox model is fit on complete cases (event_observed=1 for all rows). Living individuals were excluded from the analysable dataset rather than right-censored. This means the Cox model covers exactly the same n=8,643 as the OLS regression — both models estimate the association between migration status and age at death in the same sample.

A true survival analysis with right-censored living individuals would require future ESU follow-up data. The complete-case restriction is a limitation shared with the OLS analysis.
