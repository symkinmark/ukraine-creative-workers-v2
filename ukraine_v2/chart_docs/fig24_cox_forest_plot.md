## What this figure shows

Forest plot of hazard ratios (HR) for three migration groups (reference = non-migrated) from two Cox PH models:

- **Squares (Model 1)**: Unadjusted — migration dummies only
- **Diamonds (Model 2)**: Adjusted — migration + birth decade + profession + region

X-axis is log scale. Vertical dashed line at HR=1 (null).

- **HR < 1** = lower hazard of death = longer survival than non-migrated
- **HR > 1** = higher hazard of death = shorter survival
- Error bars = 95% confidence intervals

---

## Key values (V2.4 — right-censored N=15,053)

| Group | Model 1 HR | Model 2 HR | 95% CI (M2) | p (M2) |
|-------|-----------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Migrated | 1.088 | 0.832 | 0.778–0.889 | <0.0001 |
| Internal transfer | 1.541 | 1.105 | 1.033–1.182 | 0.0038 |
| Deported | 4.964 | 4.646 | 3.908–5.524 | <0.0001 |

Concordance (Model 2): 0.724

---

## Key finding

The migrated group shows HR > 1 in the unadjusted model but HR < 1 (0.832) in the adjusted model. This reversal is explained by the structural censoring imbalance: the non_migrated group is ~49% right-censored (living individuals who have not yet died), which artificially lowers the baseline hazard. Once birth cohort, profession, and region are controlled, the survival advantage of migrants is restored.

This demonstrates why covariate adjustment is essential in right-censored survival analysis with differential censoring across groups.

---

## PH Assumption

Schoenfeld residuals test (Model 2): PH violated for migrated (p=0.011) and deported (p<0.0001). Internal transfer borderline (p=0.066). Reported HRs represent average effects over the full follow-up period.
