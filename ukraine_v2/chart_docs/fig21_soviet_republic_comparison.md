# Fig 21 — Mean Age at Death: Our Groups vs Soviet Republic General Populations

**File:** `charts/fig21_soviet_republic_comparison.png`

## What this chart shows
External validation chart. Plots our four migration groups' mean age at death by decade of death (period data), overlaid against published life expectancy estimates for four Soviet republic groupings: Baltic SSRs, Ukrainian SSR, Russian SFSR, and Central Asian SSRs.

X-axis is death decade (1920s-1980s), clipped at 1990 (Soviet dissolution boundary). Y-axis is mean age at death / life expectancy (years).

## Key finding
- **Migrated group** tracks close to or above Baltic SSR LE — the best-performing Soviet republics. By the 1980s, migrants were dying at age 77, above the Baltic general population.
- **Non-migrated group** tracks close to Ukrainian SSR general population LE — which is expected and validates the dataset.
- **Deported group** falls below Central Asian SSR LE in early decades (the worst-performing region in the Soviet Union). Their mortality is that severe.

## What to look for
- Where our lines sit relative to the republic reference lines — this is external validation
- The non-migrated group should roughly track the Ukrainian SSR line (and it does)
- The migrated group's line rising above all Soviet-era reference lines in later decades = diaspora in the West benefiting from Western medicine and living standards
- The Great Terror & Holodomor shading (1930s-40s) — all groups dip there

## Data sources for reference lines
- Meslé & Vallin (2003) — Ukrainian/Russian LE reconstruction pre-1959
- Andreev et al. (1998) — Soviet republic comparisons
- UN WPP 2022 — post-1959 figures
- Katus et al. (2002) — Baltic SSR estimates
