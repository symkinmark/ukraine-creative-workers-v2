# Fig 07 — Death Year Distribution by Group

**File:** `charts/fig07_death_year_histogram.png`

## What this chart shows
Histogram of the calendar year of death, with each group shown as a separate colour. Shows *when* people in each group died across the 20th century.

## Key finding
- **Deported:** Deaths concentrated in 1936-1945 (Terror + WWII). Essentially done as a group by 1950.
- **Non-migrated:** Deaths spread across 1930s-1990s, peaking later (people living longer into Soviet era)
- **Migrated:** Deaths spread into the 1970s-1990s (diaspora living longer, dying later)
- **Internal transfer:** Similar pattern to non-migrated

## What to look for
- The early spike in deported deaths (1937-1943) is the Terror and WWII
- The absence of deported deaths after ~1950 = most deportees did not survive
- Migrated deaths continue long after non-migrated deaths tail off
- The gap in time represents the survival advantage being played out in calendar time
