## What this figure shows

Time-varying hazard ratios for the deported group (reference = non-migrated) estimated by fitting a separate unadjusted Cox PH model within each 10-year age band. Only individuals alive at the start of each band contribute to that band's model.

X-axis: age band midpoint. Y-axis: hazard ratio (log scale). Error bars = 95% CI. Dashed line at HR=1.0 (null).

## Key finding

The excess mortality of deported workers was not constant — it was concentrated in their thirties and forties, the ages at which most Great Terror arrests occurred and at which Gulag conditions were most lethal. Survivors who reached their seventies had essentially converged back to the non-migrated baseline (HR=0.95 at age 80–90). The single overall HR of 4.646 (fig24) is a lifetime average; this figure shows the temporal structure of that average.

---

## Historical interpretation

Workers born ~1890–1910 (the Executed Renaissance generation) were in their 30s–40s during the 1937–1938 Terror. This is exactly the peak band (40–50). The pattern is the demographic fingerprint of state-directed mass killing: a sharp spike in the risk-of-death curve at a specific historical moment, followed by collapse.

---

## PH assumption note

The Schoenfeld residuals test for the deported group (p<0.0001 in full model) flags the non-constant HR. This figure is the direct response: instead of treating this as a limitation, it is treated as information — revealing the time-structure of the excess mortality.
